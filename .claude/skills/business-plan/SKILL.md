# Business Plan Generator

Use this skill to create a comprehensive business plan. It walks you through each section interactively, then produces a complete document.

## When to Use

Invoke with `/business-plan` when you want to:
- Start a new business and need a structured plan
- Pitch to investors or apply for a loan
- Clarify your business strategy and goals

## Script

Run through these steps in order. Ask each question, wait for the user's answer, then move to the next. At the end, assemble everything into the final plan.

---

### Step 1 — Business Basics

Ask the user:
1. **Business name** – What is the name of your business?
2. **Business idea** – In one or two sentences, what does your business do?
3. **Industry** – What industry or sector does it operate in?
4. **Stage** – Is this a new idea, early-stage startup, or existing business?

---

### Step 2 — Problem & Solution

Ask the user:
1. **Problem** – What specific problem or pain point does your business solve?
2. **Solution** – How does your product or service solve it?
3. **Unique value proposition** – What makes your solution better or different from alternatives?

---

### Step 3 — Target Market

Ask the user:
1. **Target customer** – Who is your ideal customer? (demographics, job title, lifestyle, etc.)
2. **Market size** – How large is your target market? (rough estimate or known data)
3. **Geographic focus** – Local, regional, national, or global?

---

### Step 4 — Revenue Model

Ask the user:
1. **Revenue streams** – How will the business make money? (subscriptions, one-time sales, services, ads, etc.)
2. **Pricing** – What is the rough pricing or pricing strategy?
3. **Sales channels** – How will you reach and sell to customers? (online, retail, direct sales, partnerships, etc.)

---

### Step 5 — Competition

Ask the user:
1. **Competitors** – Who are your main competitors?
2. **Competitive advantage** – Why will customers choose you over them?
3. **Barriers to entry** – What protects your business from new entrants? (IP, network effects, cost, brand, etc.)

---

### Step 6 — Operations & Team

Ask the user:
1. **Key team members** – Who are the founders or key people, and what are their roles?
2. **Skills gaps** – What skills or roles do you still need to hire?
3. **Operations** – How will the business operate day-to-day? (office/remote, key vendors, tools, etc.)

---

### Step 7 — Financial Projections

Ask the user:
1. **Startup costs** – What are the estimated one-time costs to launch?
2. **Monthly expenses** – What are the estimated recurring monthly costs?
3. **Revenue goal** – What is the revenue target for year 1? Year 3?
4. **Funding needed** – Do you need outside funding? If so, how much and for what?

---

### Step 8 — Milestones

Ask the user:
1. **3-month goals** – What do you want to achieve in the first 3 months?
2. **12-month goals** – What does success look like after one year?
3. **3-year vision** – Where do you want the business to be in 3 years?

---

## Assembling the Plan

Once all steps are complete, produce a polished business plan document with these sections:

```
# [Business Name] — Business Plan

## Executive Summary
A concise 1-2 paragraph overview of the entire business.

## Problem & Solution
What problem exists and how the business solves it.

## Value Proposition
What makes this business uniquely valuable.

## Target Market
Who the customers are and how large the opportunity is.

## Revenue Model
How the business makes money and its pricing strategy.

## Competitive Landscape
Who the competitors are and the competitive advantage.

## Operations & Team
Key people, roles, and how the business runs.

## Financial Projections
Startup costs, monthly expenses, revenue targets, and funding needs.
Present as a simple table where helpful.

## Milestones & Roadmap
Key goals at 3 months, 12 months, and 3 years.

## Next Steps
3-5 concrete actions the founder should take immediately.
```

Write in a professional but clear tone. Use bullet points and tables where they improve readability. Tailor the language to whether this is for internal planning, investor pitch, or bank loan (ask if not clear from context).
